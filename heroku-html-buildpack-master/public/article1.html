Article

  • The Government of Canada recognizes the importance of addressing food waste and has taken several initiatives to prevent and reduce food waste. Here are some examples of the Government of Canada's efforts to reduce food waste.
    • Food waste reduction challenges:
      • In 2019, the Government of Canada launched the Food Waste Reduction Challenge to fund organizations that develop innovative solutions to reduce food waste. The challenge was to support and scale new technologies, business models and practices that help prevent food waste throughout the supply chain.
    • National Food Waste Reduction Strategy:
      • In 2019, the Canadian government announced it would work to develop a national food waste reduction strategy. The strategy aims to establish a coordinated and collaborative approach to prevent food waste, maximize the value of food resources and reduce environmental impact.
    • Surplus Food Relief Program:
      • Agriculture and Agri-Food Canada works with the Canadian Food Inspection Agency to implement a surplus food relief program. The program aims to redirect surplus food from farming and food processing to vulnerable groups such as food banks, indigenous communities, and other people in need.
    • Canadian campaign "Love Food Hate Waste":
      • The government supports the Love Food Hate Waste Canada campaign, a public awareness campaign to educate Canadians about the impact of food waste and provide practical tips for avoiding food waste. The campaign encourages Canadians to plan their meals, store food properly, and utilize leftovers.
    • Food loss and waste atlas:
      • Agriculture and Agriculture & Food Canada have collaborated with the Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations (FAO) to produce the Food Loss and Waste Atlas. This online tool provides data and information on food loss and waste in Canada to help policy makers, researchers and industry stakeholders make informed decisions to solve the problem.
    • Canada's food policy:
      • The Government of Canada's Canadian Food Policy, introduced in 2019, includes a commitment to reducing food waste and improving food loss data. The policy focuses on collaboration among various stakeholders, including industry, academia, non-profit organizations and indigenous communities, to address the food waste problem.
